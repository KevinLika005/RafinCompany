    Responsible construction

    Safety is part of every stage of the work

    Rafin approaches safety through preparation, clear site rules, protected working methods, machinery discipline, and continuous attention to risk prevention on every project.

    Safety principles

    A practical approach built around prevention

    Our safety focus is rooted in planning, responsible supervision, and disciplined site behavior so teams can work with clarity, protection, and operational control.

    01

    Safe working practices

    Work activities should begin with clear instructions, verified tools, and organized site conditions that support consistent and safe execution.

    02

    Site control

    Access routes, work zones, materials, and active operations must remain controlled so movement on site stays orderly and risks are reduced.

    03

    Employee protection

    Personal protective equipment, task awareness, and supervision help protect workers during routine and high-risk operations.

    04

    Machinery and equipment safety

    Machines and work equipment should be used by authorized personnel, checked regularly, and integrated into site activity with clear operational discipline.

    05

    Risk prevention

    Potential hazards are best addressed early through preparation, communication, and practical preventive measures before they affect the work.

    06

    Responsible culture

    A strong safety culture depends on responsibility at every level, from planning and supervision to daily field behavior and reporting.

    Before work starts

    Preparation and planning

    We value clear planning, review of work conditions, and coordination of people, machinery, and site access before execution begins.

    During execution

    Supervision and discipline

    Safe work depends on field supervision, proper sequencing, and disciplined compliance with site rules, protective measures, and operating limits.

    Ongoing improvement

    Attention and prevention

    We treat safety as an active process that requires observation, communication, and corrective action whenever site conditions change.
